## Table 4 — ASIL determination

| Severity class | Exposure class | C1 | C2 | C3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| S1 | E1 | QM | QM | QM |
| S1 | E2 | QM | QM | QM |
| S1 | E3 | QM | QM | A |
| S1 | E4 | QM | A | B |
| S2 | E1 | QM | QM | QM |
| S2 | E2 | QM | QM | A |
| S2 | E3 | QM | A | B |
| S2 | E4 | A | B | C |
| S3 | E1 | QM | QM | Aᵃ |
| S3 | E2 | QM | A | B |
| S3 | E3 | A | B | C |
| S3 | E4 | B | C | D |

ᵃ See 6.4.3.11.

**6.4.3.11** If several unlikely situations are combined that result in
a lower probability of exposure than E1, QM may be argued for S3, C3
based on this combination.

EXAMPLE 1 For the malfunction of a high voltage system erroneously
supplying power. The combined operational situations are:
— a crash which deploys the airbag;
— with the vehicle lying partly in the water; and
— the high voltage system partially exposed without causing an
  internal short circuit.

EXAMPLE 2 For the malfunction of a fuel pump supplying petrol
erroneously. The combined operational situations are:
— a crash which deploys the airbag;
— the tank system behind the pump remains fully functional;
— the fuel line from the pump is broken, such that petrol can drip on
  hot parts; and
— the energy supply of the pump is fully functional.

## 6.4.4 Determination of safety goals

**6.4.4.1** A safety goal shall be determined for each hazardous event
with an ASIL evaluated in the hazard analysis and risk assessment. If
similar safety goals are determined, these may be combined into one
safety goal.

NOTE Safety goals are top-level safety requirements for the item. They
lead to the functional safety requirements needed to avoid an
unreasonable risk for each hazardous event. Safety goals are not
expressed in terms of technological solutions, but in terms of
functional objectives.

**6.4.4.2** The ASIL determined for the hazardous event shall be
assigned to the corresponding safety goal. If similar safety goals are
combined into a single one, in accordance with 6.4.4.1, the highest
ASIL shall be assigned to the combined safety goal.

**6.4.4.3** The safety goals together with their ASIL shall be
specified in accordance with ISO 26262-8:2018, Clause 6.

## Corrections this makes to secondary-source claims checked earlier

A pasted secondary-source table (2026-07-16, not independently
verified before being pasted) and a follow-up "resolution" message both
turned out to conflict with the real table above once checked against
it directly:

- The original pasted table understated three cells: S2/C1/E4 (claimed
  QM, real value **A**), S3/C1/E3 (claimed QM, real value **A**), and
  S3/C1/E4 (claimed A, real value **B**).
- A third-party site (piembsystech.com) independently matched the real
  table exactly on all 12 cells (modulo simplifying the Aᵃ footnote to
  plain A) — the one secondary source checked that held up.
- Another site (nichecalcs.com) produced a table structurally different
  from the real one in multiple cells — did not hold up.
- A follow-up message claiming "C1 unconditionally defaults to QM
  regardless of severity" is directly false: the real table shows C1
  producing ASIL A at S2/E4 and S3/E3, and ASIL B at S3/E4. It also
  misstated Clause 7.4.3's real title (claimed "Allocation of
  functional safety requirements"; the real table of contents in
  `sources/ISO-26262-3-2018.pdf` says "Safety validation criteria") —
  a checkable factual error, not just an unconfirmed claim.

Full account of this verification chain: `DEVLOG.md`'s 2026-07-16 entry
for `aeb_kernel`'s ISO 26262 sourcing.
